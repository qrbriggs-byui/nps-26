Week 9 — Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS)

CLS measures unexpected movement of visible content. Lower is better: good < 0.1, poor > 0.25.

Use the buttons and watch content jump in the bad example.

Session CLS: 0.0000

What causes CLS

Bad Example

Article intro text. Scroll and click simulate.

Image loaded without reserved dimensions.

More content below that will shift downward.

How to prevent CLS

Good Example
Reserved banner space

Article intro text. Same structure, stable layout.

Image has intrinsic dimensions (space reserved).

Content below stays in place.

Checklist to reduce CLS

  • Set width and height (or aspect-ratio) on images/video/iframes.
  • Reserve fixed/min height for ads, embeds, and delayed components.
  • Avoid inserting content above existing content unless space is preallocated.
  • Use transform/opacity animations instead of layout-changing animations.
